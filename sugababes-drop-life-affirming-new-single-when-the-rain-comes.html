Sugababes Drop Life Affirming New Single 'When The Rain Comes'

Publish date: 2024-01-25

Ahead of their SOLD OUT show at The O2 Arena in London tonight (September 15th), pop titans Sugababes drop their brand new single ‘When The Rain Comes’.

Making its debut almost a decade after the release of the trios critically acclaimed comeback track ‘Flatline’, the uplifting song finds Mutya, Siobhan and Keisha in a place of gratitude, as they give thanks, and show love to those who have stood by them throughout the course of their career.

Co-written with George Moore (who also produced the track along with the group’s live band), and Iain James (Little Mix, Anne-Marie), the single is set to feature on the groups forthcoming album, which is scheduled to drop in 2024.

Commenting on the highly emotive track, the ‘Babes say in a joint statement: “We loved being back in the studio and writing this song this summer, and it feels like the perfect song to release to coincide with our biggest headline show to date. This song is a celebration of the people in our lives who stand by us and lift us up through the good and bad times. The three of us have been that for each other over the years, and we felt there was no better way to mark this moment in our journey than with this song.”
