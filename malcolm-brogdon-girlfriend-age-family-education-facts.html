Malcolm Brogdon is an American basketball player for the NBA team Indiana Pacers. He plays as a Point guard/ Shooting guard, wearing jersey number #7 for the Pacers.

Malcolm Brogdon Bio & Family; Age, Parents, Ethnicity, Height

Malcolm Moses Adams Brogdon was born on 11 December 1992 in Atlanta, Georgia, the United States that makes him 28 years of age. He is the youngest son of his parents Mitchell G. Brogdon and Jann Adams.

His father Mitchell is a lawyer and mediator by profession, whereas his mother is the Associate Dean of science and math at Morehouse College.  Malcolm is African-American by ethnicity and often speaks regarding racial equality in sports.

Brogdon’s parents divorced when he was 11 years old. He grew up with two older brothers; Gino and John Brogdon. Gino is a practicing attorney based in Atlanta, Georgia and John serves as an Associate Attorney in the City of Atlanta.

The player stands tall at a height of 6 feet and 5 inches (1.96 meters) and weighs around 230 pounds (104 kg).

Education; School, College

Malcolm pursued his high school education at Greater Atlanta Christian School in Norcross, Georgia. As a high school basketball player, he was named the All-Division Player of the Year in Georgia by the Atlanta Journal-Constitution. Similarly, the Georgia Sportswriters Association selected him as a Mr. Basketball in his senior season in 2010-11. Afterward, he went to the University of Virginia, where he began to play collegiate basketball.

The NBA player graduated from Virginia with a bachelor’s degree in history in 2015. There, he was an ACC Honor Roll member and UVa Athletic Honor Roll member at various times throughout the course of his undergraduate career. Moreover, he earned his master’s degree from VSU in leadership and public policy in 2016. He stands at a height of 6 feet 5 inches (1.96 meters) and has a muscular body with a weight of 229 lbs (104 kg).

His NBA Career; Milwaukee Bucks, Indiana Pacers

Malcolm was selected by the Milwaukee Bucks with the 36th overall pick in the 2016 NBA draft on 23 July 2016. He made his NBA debut for the Bucks against the Charlotte Hornets on 26 October 2016. For his remarkable first-season performance, he was named the 2016-17 NBA Rookie of the Year.

After playing for the Bucks for three seasons, he signed with the Indiana Pacers on 6 July 2019. Later, he became the first NBA player in history to tally at least 20 points and 10 assists in both of his first two games with the Pacers.

Recently on 25 January 2021, Malcolm improved his career-high to 36 points in the Pacers’ 129–114 win against the Toronto Raptors.
